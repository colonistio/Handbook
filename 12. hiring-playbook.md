**Execution-First. High-agency. Low Ego.**

### 1. General
We optimize for evidence of exceptional abilities.

You have acquired a certification → Okay, that's cool. 

You have built a functional tool, website or app to learn a new skill → That's amazing, we will be curious to talk to you.

All hiring decisions explicitly test for our [values](https://colonist.io/values-examples#dependability).

We don't evaluate cultural fit based on hypotheticals. We test values through recent, concrete behavior.

**You'll like Colonist if:**

- You love autonomy
- You love challenging problems
- You want to see the impact of your work fast
- You can figure things out — when you get stuck, you find ways to get unstuck and move forward
- You have the necessary discipline to work [remote-async](https://demiculus.com/remote-work/)
- You take feedback and improve
- You seek criticism and understand it's the best path for growth
- You embrace ambiguity — turning chaos into clarity motivates you

**You'll probably dislike Colonist if:**

- You want close guidance
- Challenging work is not your priority
- You want crystal clear tickets handed to you
- You just want to finish your tasks and not worry about things out of your lane
- You want a standard corporate 9-5 job and environment
- Praise is your main motivator

### 2. Hiring Signals

These apply to all roles.

- **Execution over articulation** — if a candidate cannot explain what they personally shipped, fixed, or changed, that is a weak signal
- **Recent behavior** — prefer examples from the last 6–12 months
- **Ownership beats alignment** — we value people who act, cut scope, and fix things over people who escalate or coordinate
- **Trials over speculation** — we do not hire based on potential alone
- **Clarity is the output** — interviews, tasks, and trials all answer: "Would this person raise the quality bar?" and "Will this person reduce chaos on the team?"

### 3. Main Hiring Responsibilities

#### Hiring Manager

- Shapes and signs off on the role definition
- Helps write and iterate on the job description
- Reviews applications and interview feedback
- Can run technical interviews → delegated if necessary
- Can run trials → delegated if necessary
- Owns the final hiring decision

Hiring managers are expected to spend meaningful time on hiring. This is a non-negotiable.

#### Recruiter

- Runs end-to-end hiring operations
- Opens roles and sets up the whole pipeline
- Sources and screens outstanding candidates
- Maintains process consistency
- Ensures candidate experience is amazing
- Tracks learnings and iterates the process

### 4. Role Definition (Before Opening Any Role)

Hiring needs are based on:

- Product priorities
- Team capacity
- Evidence from past hires

Every role must clearly answer:

- Why do we need this role now?
- What does success look like in 3, 6, and 12 months?
- What problems will exist if this role is not filled fast?

For each new role, we open a new issue on Linear using 'New Role Request' template. This is only available for hiring team on Linear. If it's approved, role opening starts immediately.

**Evergreen roles** — some positions, like Product Engineer, are always open. Colonist is growing fast, so for the next 1-2 years, there are some positions we'll always need. They stay open even after we hire someone. Currently:

### 5. Process

Each stage of our hiring process has a purpose, as detailed below.

#### Stage 1: Application Review

- Check must-have requirements
- Evidence of shipping
- Relevant constraints (small teams, limited resources)
- Clear written communication

#### Stage 2: Cultural Fit Interview

- Get overall picture of candidate motivation, achievements, experience and skills
- Validate real alignment with Colonist values
- Identify executors vs talkers
- Present more of Colonist to candidates

#### Stage 3: Take-Home Assignment

- Ability to follow instructions clearly
- Role's basic technical skills
- AI usage
- Clarity of thinking in async, written form
- Judgment and trade-off making
- How they receive feedback

*P.s:We're testing a new method where assignments don't exist and all technical assessment is done live. Testing with one position — if it's effective, we'll implement it across all roles.*

#### Stage 4: Technical Interview

- Practical problem-solving and technical knowledge
- Judgment
- AI usage
- How they create clarity
- Trade-offs they make under constraints
- Candidates screenshare and show their skills in action

#### Stage 5: Founder Call

- Double-check motivation, experience and achievements
- Clarify concerns from previous calls
- Reinforce cultural alignment

#### Stage 6: Trial

Trials are not onboarding. They exist to make sure we bring the right person. For 5 days, you'll work together with the Colonist team. All work is paid.

This is a good moment for you, as a candidate, to evaluate if Colonist is what you want — as much as a moment for us to evaluate fit and abilities.

### 6. Trial Design

#### Trial Principles

- Real work
- Real constraints
- Clear expectations
- Fast feedback cycles

#### Duration

1–2 weeks, depending on your availability and constraints. We understand most people reaching this stage are busy with their current jobs.

- **Ideal:** 5 consecutive full days
- **Alternative:** spread across 2 weeks
- **Last resort:** work over the weekend — though this limits interaction with the team

#### Trial Compensation

Your yearly compensation rate divided by 2,000 → this determines the hourly rate.

If you choose not to share your current salary, we'll estimate based on your experience and local market rates.

#### What We Evaluate

- Execution
- Quality
- Adaptability
- Response to feedback
- Written communication
- Alignment to our values

### 7. Decision-Making Rules

- **Strong Yes** → Hire
- **Clear No** → Stop
- **Between Yes and No** → Decide if it makes sense to extend; if not, stop

### 8. AI Usage Expectations

- AI is allowed and expected — Colonist is an AI-first company
- We evaluate how you use AI and whether it improves clarity and speed
- AI should amplify thinking, not replace ownership or judgment
- We bring in people who will level up our AI knowledge and skills

### 9. Continuous Improvement Loop

After every failed trial:

- Trial lead fills postmortem on Ashby's Trial feedback form
- Recruiter updates:
  - Role key criteria — a doc describing the ideal candidate
  - Sourcing strategy
  - Application filters
  - Trial design

If trial is a success, trial lead fills premortem → used during probation period.

### 10. Making the Decision

We decide quickly, once all evidence is collected.

After final interviews or trials:

- Interviewers submit written feedback on Ashby
- The Hiring Manager owns the final call
- Disagreements should be discussed explicitly, not averaged out

### 11. Making the Offer

Offers are made on a call with a founder.

- Cover compensation and bureaucracy (contract, starting date)
- Answer open questions

Once accepted and contract is signed, onboarding ops starts immediately.

### 12. On Feedback

Every candidate hears back from us — no exceptions.

If you don't pass the application review stage, you'll receive a brief status update without specific reasons. Yeah, I know this is not what you want to read, but that's the reality of startup hiring.

We reject people who, on paper, meet the requirements — they're just not one of the strongest people in the pool. As a small team with a high hiring bar, we have to prioritize.

If you get to the interview stages, you'll receive personalized feedback.

### 13. Interview Principles

#### Focus on Depth, Not Coverage

Go deep on a few themes rather than skimming many questions.

#### Ask for What You Need

Set expectations at the start of the interview about how it will run.

#### Name Concerns Early

For every candidate you approve, there's a premortem to be filled along with the feedback forms. Take it very seriously.

#### Seek [Specificity](https://demiculus.com/specificity/)

Details reveal the truth. Outcomes matter more than language.

#### Keep It on Track

Politely interrupt candidates if needed to maintain signal quality.

#### Feel the Weight of the Decision

Hiring has long-term consequences. Take it seriously.

Bring energy. You are part of the Colonist brand — candidates are evaluating us as much as we evaluate them.

#### Interview Scoring

For all stages, we use a 1–4 scale:

- **1 – Strong No:** Clearly not a fit
- **2 – No:** Not a fit now
- **3 – Yes:** Solid hire
- **4 – Strong Yes:** Exceptional, bar-raising candidate

Rule of thumb: between a 2 and a 3 is a 2. We do not hire "maybes".
